Who am I?

I am a second leuitenant in the U.S. Air Force and motivated by a commitement to service. Through my family's history of military service, I have learned to appreciate the sacrifices countless Americans have made to defend the values and ideals of this country.

As an engineer, I seek to use technology to make sure that all soldiers return home to their families. I believe that technology can help transition our society to become more ethical, advanced, and humane. So far my professional experiences have spanned from air and space to cyberspace. I am cosntantly focused on improving myself and learning new technologies.

As an individual, I am a pragmatists and deeply analytical. I enjoy working on interdisciplinary teams in cross-functional roles. I am currently a GEM Fellow and member of the Aerial Robotics and Experimental Autonomy Lab at Georgia Tech. I look forward to continuing to learn from others, creating new technologies, and making a positive difference in the world.
