### Building Lunar Devices 🌚

During my research I was interested in investigating the properties of lunar regolith (a.k.a. soil) as it would  help inform the stability of the moon's polar ice sheets, its internal heating mechanisms, and the requirements for future human habitation. At JPL I worked within the *Applied Low Temperature Physics Group* where I was responsible for the modeling and simulation of a cyrogenic device to measure the thermal conductivity of lunar regolith.

In my role, I was responsible for conducting **FEA simulations** to evaluate the performance of the cyrogenic instrument. My models primarily consisted of thermal analsyses using **ANSYS** and **SolidWorks** simulation tools. At the end of the project I presented my research to Caltech faculty and JPL research scientists at the Caltech research sympossium.
